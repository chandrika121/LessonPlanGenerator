Welcome back! In our last session, we learned that irrational numbers exist. Today, we will learn how to 'pin down' these elusive numbers on the number line using simple right-angled triangles. Locating Square Roots on Number Line We can locate irrational numbers like 2, 3, etc., by constructing a series of right-angled triangles. We start with a triangle where the legs are 1 unit each. Imagine walking along the number line. You can stop exactly at 1 or 2. But where is 2? We build a triangle with base 1 and height 1. Definitions Irrational Number: A number that cannot be written as a simple fraction p/q and has a non-terminating, non-repeating decimal expansion. Hypotenuse: The longest side of a right-angled triangle, opposite the 90- degree angle. Locating 5 on Number Line Problem: Construct and locate 5 on the number line using geometric construction. Given: Start with a right triangle where legs are 2 units and 1 unit. Formula: c = (a2 + b2) Steps: Draw base OA of length 2 units.; At point A, draw a perpendicular line AB of length 1 unit.; Join O to B. Solution: Identify legs: a = 2, b = 1; Apply formula: OB2 = 22 + 12; Calculate: OB2 = 4 + 1 = 5; Result: OB = 5 Explanation: By choosing legs of 2 and 1, the resulting diagonal is exactly 5 units long. We can then use a compass centered at O with radius OB to mark this point on the number line. Final answer: The length of the hypotenuse is 5 units.
