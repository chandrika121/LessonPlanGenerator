    "detailedContent": "Chemistry begins with the observation that matter behaves predictably. Antoine Lavoisier established that in any closed system, the total mass of reactants equals the total mass of products (Law of Conservation of Mass). Joseph Proust later showed that a specific compound always contains its constituent elements in a fixed proportion by mass (Law of Definite Proportions), regardless of the source. For example, water is always 1 part hydrogen to 8 parts oxygen by mass. These observations led John Dalton to propose his Atomic Theory. Furthermore, because all measurements have uncertainty, scientists use 'Significant Figures' to communicate how precise a value is. Non-zero digits are significant; leading zeros are not; trailing zeros after a decimal point are."
