    "detailedContent": "When light travels from air (a rarer medium) into glass or water (denser media), it slows down. This change in speed causes the wavefront to pivot, resulting in bending towards the normal line drawn at the point of incidence. Conversely, when light exits a denser medium like glass back into air, it speeds up and bends away from the normal. A common example is a straw placed in a glass of water; the part under water appears bent or raised because the light rays coming from that part bend as they leave the water to enter our eyes."
