    "detailedContent": "When Magnesium burns, it reacts with oxygen to form Magnesium Oxide (MgO), which is basic. When Sulphur burns, it forms Sulphur Dioxide (SO2), which is acidic. This difference helps us classify substances. Furthermore, metals have a hierarchy called the Reactivity Series. A metal higher in the series can displace a metal lower in the series from its salt solution. For example, Zinc displaces Copper from Copper Sulphate solution because Zinc is more reactive. However, Copper cannot displace Zinc. This concept is crucial for understanding corrosion and metal extraction."
