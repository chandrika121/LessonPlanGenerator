The general formula for saturated monohydric alcohols is CnH2n+1OH.\nThe carbon bearing the -OH group is sp3 hybridized. This geometry allows for free rotation and distinct physical properties like high boiling points due to intermolecular hydrogen bonding.\n*Classification:* Based on the number of alkyl groups attached to the carbon holding the OH:\n- Primary (1°): Carbon holds 0 or 1 other C.\n- Secondary (2°): Carbon holds 2 other Cs.\n- Tertiary (3°): Carbon holds 3 other Cs.\n\n**2. Phenols (Ar-OH)**\nPhenols are compounds where the hydroxyl group is directly attached to a benzene ring. The carbon atom holding the -OH is sp2 hybridized and part of the aromatic system.\nThe direct attachment leads to resonance effects that make phenols more acidic than alcohols, though we will explore this reactivity in future sessions. For now, focus on structure: Benzene Ring + OH = Phenol family.\n\n**3.
