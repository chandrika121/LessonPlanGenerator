    "detailedContent": "In Class IX, we move beyond simple storytelling. We treat Social Science as a discipline of inquiry. For instance, when studying 'Democracy', it's not just about memorizing who was elected (History) or where the elections were held (Geography). It is about understanding *why* democracy emerged in certain places at certain times and how laws are made to protect rights (Civics). \n\nThe key difference from Natural Sciences is the nature of evidence. A chemist can repeat an experiment 100 times to get a result. A social scientist cannot 'repeat' the French Revolution or create a controlled environment for human migration. Therefore, they rely on primary sources (letters, laws), secondary analysis, and statistical data. This requires critical evaluation: Is this source biased? Who wrote it? What is missing?\n\nFurthermore, real-world problems are rarely isolated to one subject. Climate change involves geographical patterns of weather, historical industrialization trends, and civic policies for regulation. Understanding Social Science means learning to weave these threads together."
