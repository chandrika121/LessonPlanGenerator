    "detailedContent": "The concept of electric charge begins with the atom. Protons carry a positive charge (+e), and electrons carry a negative charge (-e). When an object gains electrons, it becomes negatively charged; when it loses electrons, it becomes positively charged. A crucial property is quantization: you cannot have half an electron's charge. The total charge Q on any body is always an integral multiple of the elementary charge e (1.6 x 10^-19 C). Another key principle is conservation: in an isolated system, the net charge remains constant; it can only be transferred, not created or destroyed. Finally, we introduce the Electric Field. A charge creates a region around it called the electric field. If another charge enters this region, it feels a force. We visualize this using field lines: they start from positive charges and end at negative charges. The density of these lines indicates the strength of the field."
