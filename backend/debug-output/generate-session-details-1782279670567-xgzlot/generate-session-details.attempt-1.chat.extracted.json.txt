    "detailedContent": "We begin with the Bar Magnet as a magnetic dipole. The torque $\\tau = mB \\sin \\theta$ acts to align the moment $m$ with the field $B$. We discuss Magnetic Permeability ($\\mu$) and Susceptibility ($\\chi_m$). For diamagnetic materials (e.g., Bismuth), induced dipoles oppose the external field. Paramagnetic materials (e.g., Aluminum) have permanent dipoles that align partially with the field, overcoming thermal agitation. Ferromagnetic materials (Iron, Nickel) exhibit domains; below the Curie temperature ($T_c$), these domains align spontaneously to create strong magnetism. Finally, we model Earth as a dipole tilted at 11.3° from the rotational axis, defining Declination (angle between magnetic and geographic meridian) and Inclination/Dip (vertical angle of field lines)."
