    "keyPoints": [

    "detailedContent": "A Rational Number includes all integers (like -2, 0, 5) because they can be written as -2/1, etc. When we divide the numerator by the denominator of a rational number, two things happen: either the remainder becomes zero eventually (Terminating Decimal), or the remainders start repeating in a cycle (Recurring Decimal). For example, $1/4 = 0.25$ (terminates) and $1/3 = 0.33...$ (repeats).\n\nIn contrast, Irrational Numbers like $\sqrt{2}$, $\pi$, or $e$ cannot be written as a simple fraction. Their decimal expansions go on infinitely without ever settling into a repeating pattern. This makes them 'incommensurable' with integers in terms of exact fractional representation."
