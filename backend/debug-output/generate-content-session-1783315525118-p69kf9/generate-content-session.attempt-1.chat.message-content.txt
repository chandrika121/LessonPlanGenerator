    "detailedContent": "The concept of electric charge explains phenomena ranging from lightning to the stability of atoms. When we rub a glass rod with silk, electrons move from the glass to the silk. The glass becomes positively charged (lost electrons), and the silk becomes negatively charged (gained electrons). The total charge remains zero. This is the Law of Conservation of Charge. Furthermore, charge is not continuous; it comes in discrete packets called 'quanta'. The smallest unit is the charge of an electron (e = 1.6 x 10^-19 C). Any observable charge q must be an integer multiple of e (q = ne). This quantization is usually not visible in daily life because the number of electrons involved is huge, making the charge appear continuous. However, at the atomic level, it is strictly discrete."
