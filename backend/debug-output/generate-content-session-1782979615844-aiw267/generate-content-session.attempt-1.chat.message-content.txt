Phenols, however, are derivatives of benzene where one hydrogen is replaced by -OH. This structural difference (sp3 vs sp2/aromatic) makes phenols more acidic and less reactive towards nucleophilic substitution compared to alcohols in many contexts.\n\nNomenclature follows strict IUPAC rules: The parent chain must contain the carbon with the OH group. Phenols are named as derivatives of 'phenol' where position 1 is always the carbon with OH.\n\nClassification is crucial for predicting reactivity. A primary alcohol has the C-OH attached to one other carbon (e.g., Ethanol), secondary to two (e.g., Isopropanol), and tertiary to three (e.g., tert-Butyl alcohol). This classification dictates how they undergo oxidation, dehydration, and substitution in subsequent lessons."
