    "detailedContent": "Electric charge is not a new substance but a property of matter, similar to mass. Just as mass attracts other masses via gravity, charge interacts with other charges via electrostatic force. The magnitude of this force is governed by Coulomb's Law: F = k(q1q2)/r^2. This means the force increases if charges get bigger and decreases rapidly if they move apart (inverse square law). Furthermore, charge cannot be created or destroyed; it can only be transferred from one body to another (Conservation of Charge). Also, charge comes in discrete packets called electrons; you cannot have half an electron's worth of charge (Quantization). The region around a charge where it exerts force on another charge is called the Electric Field."
