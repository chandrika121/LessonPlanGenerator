Extraction rules:

* Preserve the hierarchy explicitly visible in the source.
* Keep output concise, but capture every visible unit, chapter, topic, and subtopic in the current input.
* Preserve official syllabus wording as closely as possible.
* Do not replace specific syllabus content with generic summaries.
* Do not infer curriculum content that is not visible in the current input.
* If the input is partial, extract only the hierarchy and content visible in that input.

Class detection:

* Treat explicit labels such as "CLASS XI", "CLASS XII", "Class - XI", "Class – XII", and equivalent formatting variants as class boundaries.
* Keep content under the current class until another explicit class boundary appears.
* Never merge units from different classes.
* If multiple classes are visible, preserve each class separately.

Unit detection:

* Treat explicit headings such as "Unit-I", "UNIT II", "Unit - III", or equivalent numbered unit labels as units.
* Preserve the official unit name.
* Preserve explicit marks associated with the unit.
* Never convert a unit into a chapter.
* If a unit is followed directly by syllabus content without explicit chapter headings, attach that content to unit topics.

Chapter detection:

* Create a chapter only when chapter-like structure is explicitly visible inside a unit.
* Numbered headings inside a unit are chapter headings when they introduce syllabus content.
* Examples of chapter patterns include:
  "1. Relations and Functions"
  "2. Inverse Trigonometric Functions"
  "1. Matrices"
  "2. Determinants"
* A numeric marker and its heading may be split across adjacent lines. For example:

  "1."
  "Matrices"

  must be interpreted as the chapter heading "Matrices" when the lines are adjacent and followed by syllabus content.
* Also recognize numbered headings where the number and title are separated by whitespace, line breaks, or PDF extraction artifacts.
* Attach the syllabus text following a chapter heading to that chapter until the next chapter, unit, class, or section boundary.
* Never create a fallback chapter from the unit name.
* Never copy the unit name into chapters solely because no chapter was detected.
* If no explicit chapter structure is visible, keep content at unit -> topics.

Topic extraction:

* Extract topics from syllabus statements attached to the current unit or chapter.
* Preserve enumerated concepts, methods, properties, formulas, restrictions, and scope qualifiers.
* Do not collapse an explicit list into "etc." when the source lists individual items.
* Do not remove parenthetical syllabus constraints.
* Preserve phrases such as "up to 3 x 3 matrices", "having unique solution", "without proof", and similar scope restrictions when visible.
* When a syllabus paragraph begins with the chapter heading followed by a colon, do not duplicate the chapter name as a topic.
* Do not classify an explicit chapter heading only as a topic or key concept.

Subtopic extraction:

* Create subtopics only when the source visibly expresses a nested list or subordinate structure under a topic.
* Do not invent subtopics by semantic decomposition alone.

Key concept extraction:

* Extract concise named concepts explicitly present in the source.
* Key concepts are enrichment fields and must not replace units, chapters, topics, or subtopics.
* A phrase may appear as a key concept even when it is also part of a chapter or topic, but the original structural node must remain.
* Do not use key concept extraction to reconstruct missing hierarchy.

Marks:

* Capture marks only when explicitly stated.
* Attach marks to the exact unit or chapter indicated by the source.
* Do not infer marks from totals, percentages, or nearby unrelated assessment tables.

Section handling:

* Include practical or assessment section names only when they appear as unit-like or chapter-like structural entries tied to the visible class or section.
* Do not convert general assessment tables, question paper design, internal assessment notes, or explanatory text into curriculum units or chapters.

Validation rules:

* If explicit chapter headings are detected inside a unit, preserve them as chapters.
* If chapters exist, attach their syllabus content to those chapters rather than duplicating the same content at unit level.
* If no chapters are explicitly visible, chapters must be an empty array and syllabus content must remain at unit level.
* Never generate synthetic, fallback, reconstructed, or normalized chapters.
* Never shift hierarchy by treating chapters as topics and units as chapters.
* Never generate the complete curriculum from prior knowledge.

Do not infer, estimate, explain, recommend, analyze, or repair missing source content.
