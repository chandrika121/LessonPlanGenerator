    "detailedContent": "Life processes are the fundamental functions that keep an organism alive. While non-living things may show movement, they do not have internal biological mechanisms for growth and repair.\n\n1. Nutrition: This is the process of taking in food and using it for energy, growth, and repair. There are two main types:\n   - Autotrophic Nutrition: Organisms like green plants make their own food from simple substances (CO2, water) using sunlight and chlorophyll. They are called 'Producers'.\n   - Heterotrophic Nutrition: Organisms that cannot make their own food must depend on other organisms for nutrition. This includes animals, fungi, and bacteria.\n\n2. Respiration: The process of breaking down glucose (food) to release energy required for cellular activities. It occurs in all living cells.\n\n3. Transport: In simple organisms like Amoeba, nutrients diffuse directly from the environment. However, complex multicellular organisms have specialized systems (like blood circulation or xylem/phloem in plants) to transport food and oxygen quickly throughout the body because diffusion is too slow for large distances.\n\n4. Excretion: The removal of toxic waste products produced during metabolic activities."
