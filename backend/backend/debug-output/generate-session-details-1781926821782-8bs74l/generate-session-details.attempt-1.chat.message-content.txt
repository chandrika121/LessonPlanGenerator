    "detailedContent": "We begin by classifying matter. Pure substances (elements like Iron or compounds like Water) have fixed melting/boiling points and cannot be separated physically. Mixtures retain the properties of their components. We explore heterogeneous mixtures (sand in water) where particles settle, versus homogeneous solutions (salt in water). A critical concept is the Tyndall effect: light scattering by colloidal particles (like milk or fog), which distinguishes them from true solutions. Separation techniques are then detailed: Filtration for insoluble solids; Evaporation/Distillation for soluble salts and liquids based on volatility differences; Chromatography for separating pigments in ink based on solubility rates. Finally, we connect this to 'Atoms and Molecules' by explaining that while compounds have fixed mass ratios (Law of Definite Proportions), alloys like Brass are mixtures where the ratio can vary."
