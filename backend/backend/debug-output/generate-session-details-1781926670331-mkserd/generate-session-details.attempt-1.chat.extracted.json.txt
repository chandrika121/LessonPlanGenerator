    "detailedContent": "We begin by defining matter. Unlike non-matter (like thoughts or light), matter has mass and volume. The particle model explains why solids are rigid (strong forces, fixed positions) while gases expand to fill containers (weak forces, high kinetic energy). We discuss diffusion—the mixing of particles due to random motion—and how it is faster in liquids than solids but slower than gases. Next, we differentiate pure substances (elements and compounds with definite melting/boiling points) from mixtures (homogeneous like salt water vs heterogeneous like sand and iron filings). The concept of latent heat explains why ice melts at 0°C without getting hotter until fully melted."
